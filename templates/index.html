The Traitors

How to Play

The Traitors is a game of trust, betrayal and deduction. At the start, every player is secretly assigned to one of two teams: the Faithful, who are the many, and the Traitors, who are the few. Only the Traitors know who the Traitors are.

Each day the whole group competes in tasks and gathers at the round table to banish whomever they suspect. Each night the Traitors quietly murder Faithfuls. The Faithful win if every Traitor has been eliminated by the time the Final ends. The Traitors win if even one of them is still standing at the end of the Final.

Game Flow

The game runs over three days, each with a task, a round table, and a night. After the third night, eight finalists compete in a final task before the final round table.

Roles

Faithful make up the majority. They do not know who anyone else is. Their only weapons are their instincts and their votes: watch closely, argue your case at the round table, and banish the Traitors before they pick you off. The Faithful win by eliminating every last Traitor.

Traitors are the few. They know one another's identities and act as a team. Each night they murder a Faithful of their choosing. By day they must blend in, avoid suspicion, and steer the round table toward the innocent. A single surviving Traitor at the end of the Final is enough for them to take the win.

Tasks & Shields

Each day opens with a task or tasks overseen by the hosts. Everyone takes part — even eliminated players — but only those still in the game can win a shield. After a team has won a task and been awarded a shield, they must choose who among them will receive the shield.

A shield is single-use and lasts one night only: it protects its holder from the coming night's murder and then expires. Shields are public — everyone can see who holds one. Remember, a Traitor may still want to win a shield in order to appear to be a Faithful.

Round Table

After each task the group gathers at the round table to debate. When the talking is done, every player still in the game casts a vote to banish someone. The players with the most votes are banished and must reveal their true allegiance to the group before they leave — Faithful or Traitor.

Multiple players will be banished at each round table. If a vote ties, the hosts call a run-off between the tied players. A banished player is out of the game and can no longer win shields or vote, but is welcome to keep taking part in the tasks.

Night

Each night the Traitors meet in secret and choose multiple Faithfuls to murder. A player holding a shield that night cannot be murdered.

Come morning, the murdered are revealed. Like the banished, a murdered player is out of the game — no more votes, no more shields — but is welcome to stay and take part in the tasks for the rest of the event.

The Final

After the third night, eight finalists remain. They first face the final task, the winner of which will earn a unique prize.

The finalists then sit for the final round table. Here the voting is iterated: round after round they choose to banish or to end the game. The moment the finalists vote to end, the game resolves. If every Traitor has been rooted out, the Faithful share the win; if even one Traitor is still seated, the Traitors take everything.
